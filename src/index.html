what is the heart?


The heart is an organ of the human body and many animal species. It is responsible for the functioning of the blood and lymph transport system.
Its walls consist of muscles, that contract and relax, pumping blood around the body 24/7.

what is its function?


The main task of the heart is to pump blood all over the body.

The blood transports and provides oxygen, minerals and vitamins that allow the body to move, grow and repair itself.
Simultaneously, the blood collects the accumulated waste, taking it to the the lungs, kidneys and the liver, which are responsible for the disposal.

what does it look like?


The heart is about the size of a fist and is located behind the breastbone, slightly on the left.

Inside, the heart is divided into four sections (see the illustration).
The atriums (the two upper parts) communicate with the ventricles (the two lower parts) through valves. The valves work so that the blood can flow from the atrium to the ventricle but not vice versa.

right chamber


The right side of the heart, contracting, recalls the blood that circulates in the veins of the body and sends it to the lungs through the vena cava.

The blood is dark red.

recharge in the lungs


The blood that reaches the lungs is full of waste and of the carbon dioxide (CO2) that has been collected in the various organs. CO2 gets breathed out/exhaled when it reaches the lungs.
In the lungs an exchange takes place: The blood is filled with the oxygen (O2) that comes from the breathed air and then returns to the left side of the heart.

left chamber


The blood that reaches the lungs is full of waste and of the carbon dioxide (CO2) that has been collected in the various organs. The waste gets breathed out when it reaches the lungs.

did you know that...


In a minute the human heart beats between 60 and 80 times, while that of an elephant from 20 to 30.

In a minute the human heart pumps about 5 liters of blood.
When we run or when we are under stress, the beats increase and can even reach 150 - 200 per minute and the blood circulation goes up to 8 liters.

Over the course of a lifetime, around 200 million liters of blood circulate in our body.
